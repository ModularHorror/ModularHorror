Issue Zero: Act I — Upstairs

I’d just gotten upstairs when I heard the front door swing open.

My parents were laughing—really laughing, the kind they save for her. My little sister, Nina.

They were praising her again, voices rising like applause. Something about a drawing. Something about putting it on the fridge.

“Come down and see it!”

I didn’t move. Not yet.

We’d had a moment earlier. Not a fight, exactly. Just... a shift.

I stayed in the hallway, listening. The house felt split—between the celebration downstairs and the quiet up here.

I told myself I’d go down later. Maybe.

Instead, I drifted toward Nina’s room. The door was cracked, just enough to see inside.

Her dolls were lined up on the shelf. Every single one had its hands over its eyes, half of the peek-a-boo ritual complete.

Ritual? Where did I get that from?

One doll was turned toward the door. Its hands weren’t quite aligned.

I raised my own hands. Just to see.

The hallway felt… deeper.

I didn’t touch them. I didn’t say anything. I just backed away.

And then I remembered this morning.

I’d woken up to the sound of breathing. Not loud. Just close.

Nina was standing at the foot of my bed.

Her hands were over her eyes.

I finally went downstairs.

The drawing was already on the fridge.

It looked… cheerful. To them.

To me, the sun had too many rays. The house had no door. The balloon string was frayed.

My parents were smiling like it was the best thing they’d ever seen.

Nina pointed to the magnet holding it up.

“Look what they got me for doing so well!” she said.

It was a yellow magnet. Round. With a cartoon face.
It said: ModularHorror.com
